Tomato Sauce Recipe

Description

Tomato sauce is a rich, savory base made by simmering ripe tomatoes with onions, garlic, and herbs like basil and oregano. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to deepen, creating a smooth, tangy, and slightly sweet sauce that can be used in countless dishes. A splash of olive oil adds extra richness, while salt and pepper bring everything together.

This classic sauce is incredibly versatile and pairs perfectly with pasta, lasagna, pizza, or even as a dipping sauce. You can customize it by adding vegetables, chili flakes for heat, or a touch of sugar to balance acidity. Homemade tomato sauce not only tastes fresher but also fills your kitchen with a warm, comforting aroma.

Ingredients

  • Tomatoes
  • Butter and Olive oil
  • Vegetables
  • Wine
  • Seasoning and herbs
  • Tomato paste

Steps

  1. Boil the tomatoes until the skin starts to peel, then transfer them to an ice bath.
  2. Slip off the skins and squeeze out the seeds.
  3. Chop eight tomatoes, then puree them in a blender. Chop the remaining tomatoes and set aside.
  4. Saut√© the veggies and garlic until very tender. Add the pureed and chopped tomatoes.
  5. Stir in the wine, basil, and seasoning. Add the celery stalks and bay leaf.
  6. Simmer for two hours, stir in the tomato paste, then continue simmering for two more hours.
  7. Remove the bay leaf and celery before serving.
