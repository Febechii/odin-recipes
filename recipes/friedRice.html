Fried Rice Recipe

Description

Fried rice is a quick and flavorful dish made by stir-frying cooked rice with vegetables, eggs, and your choice of protein like chicken, shrimp, or tofu. The combination of soy sauce, garlic, and sesame oil gives it a savory and slightly smoky taste that’s both satisfying and comforting. It’s a perfect way to use up leftover rice and turn it into a delicious meal.

The beauty of fried rice lies in its versatility—you can add almost any ingredient you have on hand, from peas and carrots to bell peppers or even pineapple for a sweet twist. Each spoonful delivers a mix of textures, from the soft grains of rice to the crunchy vegetables. Serve it hot as a main dish or a side, and enjoy its simple, homemade goodness.

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup chopped baby carrots
  • ½ cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced, or to taste (Optional)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups leftover cooked and chilled white rice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce, or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil, or to taste

Steps

  1. Assemble Ingredients
  2. Place carrots in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a low boil and cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in peas, then immediately drain in a colander.
  3. Heat a wok over high heat. Pour in vegetable oil, then stir in carrots, peas, and garlic; cook for about 30 seconds.   4. Stir in cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat. Drizzle with sesame oil and toss again.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
